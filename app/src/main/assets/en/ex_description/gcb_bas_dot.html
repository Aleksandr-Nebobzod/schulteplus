The dot

This simple yet powerful exercise utilizes the "Base" space, the main task here is pure concentration on the object, indirectly solving the problem of acquaintance with reactive movements of the mind.

Instruction

Perceive only the dot for 3-5 minutes. Try to perceive only the dot. Try not to pay attention to visual effects, thoughts, and other signals (sounds, sensations, emotions).
At some point, we discover that the main object of perception has become an imaginary plot; we need to register the event:

  1. Press the right button of the "Base" space;
  2. Try to reconstruct (step by step backwards in time) the chain of associations that led us to the perception of the imaginary plot;
  3. Perceive only the dot...

Upon completing the exercise, press the left button of the "Base" space, fill out an entry in the journal.

Summary

An example of attention absorption by perception[1] can be watching a suspenseful movie or engaging in a heated debate, but the dot is an extremely uninformative object and attention will seek many reasons to interrupt the perception process. Learning to notice moments of attention shift and restoring focus is one of the main tasks at this initial stage.

[1] In describing practices, we do not aim for scientific rigor. Here, it means that the process of perception (of anything) has taken exclusive rights to the function of attention, depriving the subject of awareness. In psychology, a similar phenomenon is described as "post-voluntary attention".
