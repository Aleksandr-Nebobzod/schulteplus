Khao Manee

This exercise utilizes the "Base" space, the main task here is pure concentration on the object, indirectly solving the problem of acquainting oneself with the reactive movements of the mind.

Instruction

In this exercise, you must duplicate the dots: gradually obtaining four dots, then three. Physically, your eyes should look as if into the distance[1], focusing on the central dot and maintaining attention on it.
Steps from the previous exercise are applicable. Additionally, during the session, it is important to realize that:

  1. The middle dot is a non-existent object;
  2. Other semantic contents are also non-existent objects;
  3. Perceive only the dot...

Upon completing the exercise, press the left button of the "Base" space, fill out an entry in the journal.
Realize that despite the illusory nature of objects in this world, we live in this world, interact, and can be productive and happy.

Summary

Initial encounters with illusions allow for a more fluid perception of reality, more finely noticing the fluctuations of the mind, and with gentle volitional effort, directing our energy towards what we consider beneficial for ourselves and others.

[1] A version of this exercise with eye convergence is possible, where the focus of the eyes is closer than the stimulus material (eyes are converged inward).
