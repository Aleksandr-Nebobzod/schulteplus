Training Space "Schulte"

Questions about measuring mental state have long been a mystery for philosophers and continue to be of interest to modern scientists. At the beginning of the 20th century, German psychiatrist Walter Schulte (Wikipedia (Deutsch). W. Schulte) developed the Schulte Tables — a psychodiagnostic method for studying and developing attention properties. (Handwiki (English). Schulte tables)
The development effect in his work is particularly significant, as it is quantitatively measurable, and thus objective. Many researchers confirm the positive impact of such training and use it for general development (including children, under adult supervision of course) and for specific tasks such as speed reading skills.

The space of "Schulte" represents an original stimulus material in the form of a table, with elements[1] of sequences placed randomly in the cells but need to be opened in an orderly manner:

  • 5x5 One sequence[2], increasing, for example:
    1, 2, 3, 4, ... 25; or: A, B, C, D.... or I, II, III, IV, V, VI,,,,
  • 7x7 Two sequences, one by one: increasing blue and decreasing red[3]. Example:
    1b, 24r; 2b, 23r; 3b, 22r; 4b, 22r; ... 24b; 1r, 25b;
  • 10x10 Four sequences, step by step changing next color: increasing blue, decreasing red, converging green: diverging yellow:
    1b, 25r, 1g, 13y;
    2b, 24r, 25g, 14y;
    3b, 23r, 2g, 12y;
    4b, 22r, 24g, 15y,
    ... 25b, 1r, 13g, 1y;
Instruction

The feature of working with Schulte tables is the calm, rhythmic use of attention without pronounced events. Strive to perceive the entire table, along with its changes, as a single whole process. The following techniques help us with this:

  • Peripheral vision. That is, during the search for the next element, try to keep the gaze in the center of the table.
  • Silent understanding. Strive to relax the speech apparatus and the corresponding function of consciousness so as not to interfere with attention doing its full work.
  • Process goal-setting. It is important to perform regular training. Achievement milestones will come by themselves; sooner or later it will become noticeable. Whereas the competitive aspect generally takes a resource away from the practitioner.
    (just do not do things like: "well, I am moving faster now than last time", "oh, another mistake!", "today I will beat Vasya, Masha,.." and so on)
    Only the process. In a sense, even "I" is absent here. Let "I" return after the training and write a useful note.
Controls
  1. status bar[4] of the exercise — displays numerical execution parameters
    • Next expected element, Total turns, Total errors, Execution time.
  2. center of the screen — grid field with cells; the cells contain all elements of the sequence without repetitions, one cell contains the next element;
    • clicking on the correct cell advances the exercise to the next status[5];
    • clicking on an incorrect cell does not advance the exercise to the next status, increases the error counter, and highlights the clicked cell;
    • long pressing any cell highlights the correct cell (hint of the next sequence element);
  3. when correctly clicking on the last element of the sequence, the program calls the "Summary" dialog, which contains the exercise results and offers:
    • repeat the exercise with the [OK] button
    • end the exercise without filling out the self-observation journal — button [heLL][6] and return to the main screen.
    • enter a self-observation note: a) impressions, achievements, observations, and future questions; b) emotional state; c) energy state)
      -- button changes label to [No][7] and return to the main screen.
  4. system button [◁] "interrupt the exercise" (on different Android devices, the "back" function may be implemented differently), calls the "Summary" dialog, which does not contain results and offers:
    • continue the exercise with the [OK] button
    • end the exercise — button [heLL][6]

Self-Monitoring Journal

Summarizing each exercise, you can maintain and analyze the history of self-observations, various aspects of your overall condition, and during practices. This can help track your progress, development pace, and adjust efforts according to your state.

Attention! Personal note:
10-30 minutes a day is a general recommendation for the duration of sessions with Schulte tables. Choose your training pace mindfully, listening to your individuality. Schulte tables are a high-intensity trainer.
In case of a sustained (3-5 days) decline in results, especially if emotional and energy levels are also reduced, we strongly recommend:

  • stopping Schulte table training;
    (you can replace them with less intense exercises)
  • allocating time for walks in the fresh air;
  • normalizing sleep patterns;
  • normalizing dietary patterns;
After a week of rest and recovery of attention resources, you can carefully try Schulte table training again. If the psyche again responds with noticeable resistance, we recommend choosing another trainer (words, colored balls, basic exercises).
Take care of yourself! You are very much needed by yourself!

Startup Settings
  • Primary Flow;
  • Ice and Fire;
  • Four Seasons;
  • Mishmash! (in progress);
    -- choose exercise type

  • Ratings
    • off: Most of options are available, this is a personal training mode.
    • on: Your options are limited and comparable to other participants. Your results can be recorded on the Wall of fame.

    • Options. (section unavailable when Ratings are enabled, except for font settings)

  • Status. Highlights, reminders, and timing are interesting for beginners.
  • Shuffle, but don't stir! These exercises have different goals:
    • off: a static table is more aimed at visual memory and attention work with the internal reflection of reality,
    • on: a dynamic table changes after each move, more focused on attention switching and external work.
  • Table Width or X-size
  • Table Height or Y-size
  • Squares
    • off: the table maintains square cells
    • on: the table tries to occupy the entire screen
  • Type of symbol in the cell. Offers different sequences:

    • • Number Arabic
    • Number Roman
    • Letter Latin
    • Letter Cyrillic
    • Letter Devanagari
    • Color Red
    • Color Blue
  • Probabilities (section allows managing the behavior of random element distribution in the table, unavailable when Ratings are enabled)
    • off: Uniform distribution. Equal likelihood of an element appearing in any cell.
    • on: Controlled distribution. The probability of an element appearing depends on the color density in the image.
    • Colors indicate:
    • White -- almost impossible
    • Yellow -- unlikely
    • Green -- likely
    • Blue -- probably
  • Small probabilities are reset (feature in development)
    • off: not zero, meaning the next element may occasionally appear in the white area
    • on: zero, the next element will definitely not appear in the white area
  • Distribution shape
    • Density distribution parameter over the surface (hump or doughnut)
    • Horizontal offset
    • Vertical offset
  • Font size
    • Small, normal, large
